# Turing Interview Preparation Strategy

Turing's process for senior candidates focuses heavily on your ability to drive technical impact and manage high-growth client expectations.

## The elevator Pitch (IA Pattern)
Focus on **Results** first, then **Action**, then **Context**.
- *Example:* "I reduced P99 latency by 40% (Result) by implementing a distributed rate-limiting system using Redis and Lua (Action) for a platform processing 500M emails monthly (Context)."

## Key Metrics to Mention
- **Scale**: 100k+ RPS, 1B+ data points.
- **Velocity**: Reducing deploy times from hours to minutes.
- **Reliability**: Maintaining 99.99% uptime during sharding migrations.

## Final Interview Tips
1. **Think Aloud**: Interviewer cares more about the *path* than the answer.
2. **Clarify Constraints**: Ask about team size, budget, and time-to-market before proposing a solution.
3. **Be Opinionated**: Staff engineers are expected to say "No" to premature microservices or complex sharding if a simpler solution exists.
